### Discussion on the interview protocol          

**The use of the word responsibility "who is responsible?":**  
- Wolter: "who is responsible does not say anything about who is doing the actual work", responsibility can mean a lot of things (taxonomy of responsibility, moral responsibility, legal responsibility etc.).
  - Wolter: We were also interested in who is doing the work, not just who is responsible. 
  - Simon: This means, IT security guy might be responsible to deal with cyber threats, but if there is something in the OT, the IT security guy on paper will (be responsible) to guide the OT teams, and OT needs to actually do the job. (Thought: IT security guy might not know anything about OT, and that is what makes this whole IT-OT coordination challenging). 
  - Simon: It is not OT's primary job to deal with cyber, and this is what makes all the coordination difficult. 
  - Wolter: so we are basically trying to understand "how is it organized?"
  - Simon: you do say, what core operations does this infrastructure supports, but not the actual tasks are?
  - Simon: implicitly it can be thought that "what are the tasks?", is covered in the question about "what role" but that is the person, not what the organization does. 
  - Simon: There is a question missing between questions 2 and 3, which is "what is the work involved in managing cyber threats in OT infrastructure?"
  - simon: how is that monitored and organized, and who is responsible for what of the task you have mentioned? 
  - So the flow would be, with this additional question, they might have listed some tasks, and then we say, who is actually responsible for what of the tasks, and who actually takes action on those tasks?
  - Wolter: replace "who is responsible" with "who is involved?"
  - Ronak: so should I add the question? Regarding what work is involved in dealing with cyber threats with OT infrastructure.        
  - Simon and Wolter: it would still make a lot of sense, provided that when we use the term "cybersecurity monitoring", it might mean different things for different people, it is not standardized like "when we say open the door". 

**Daily monitoring or incident-specific monitoring**
- Simon: It should be “for “cybersecurity and not “of “cybersecurity if the objective is to target monitoring with respect to “daily monitoring”, and not incident-specific monitoring.

**Missing the angle of coordination**  
- Wolter: so now we do have who is involved, and what are the reasons for organizing it this way? But who is involved does not give the whole picture about how is it organized? Organization involves coordination and things like that.
- Simon: it does not say, how are they involved?  

**Why the question on training
**
- Simon: The question on training assumes that someone is getting trained. Only then the follow-up questions or probes are valid.   
- Simon: We found in the Tennet study, that this is not the case, they playbook everything, then why would we assume that someone is getting trained? 
- Ronak: May be there is a mix up between using the term training and exercising? 
- Simon: It would make more sense to ask how do they prepare IT security to manage OT and the other way round, and then ask the probes, is it using training, or scenarios or what? 
- Simon: how do IT security staff get prepared to coordinate with OT staff, and the other way round? 

**How to manage the interview in 60 minutes**    
- Simon: prioritize the interview question, based on what research question are we targeting?  
- Simon: probes are nice, but they are not interview questions. They should assist in getting targeted information if the organic information is not enough. 
   
  
## Decisions

- Critically think about the questions, and align the objective of the study, to the questions I am asking    
- Make the changes in use of the word responsibility, add the question on what work is involved in managing cyber threats in OT infrastructure, think through the question on training, and adding the context of coordination when we want to know about how it is organized?
